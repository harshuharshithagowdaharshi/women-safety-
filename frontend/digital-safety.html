Digital Cyber Safety

Protect yourself online with strategies to ensure your digital privacy and prevent cyber harassment.

Digital Privacy Protection

Passwords

Create unique, complex passwords for all accounts. Use a combination of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters. Consider using a password manager to keep track of them.

Two-Factor Authentication

Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) whenever possible. This adds an extra layer of security beyond just a password, making it harder for attackers to access your accounts.

Privacy Settings

Regularly review and update privacy settings on all social media platforms. Limit who can see your posts, personal information, and location data to trusted connections only.

Online Safety Information

Beware of Phishing

Be cautious of suspicious emails, messages, or links, even if they appear to come from trusted sources. Never share personal information unless you're certain of the recipient's identity.

Digital Footprint

Remember that everything you post online can potentially be permanent and public. Think carefully before sharing personal information, photos, or your location.

Block and Report

If you encounter harassment, immediately block the person and report the behavior to the platform. Keep evidence by taking screenshots before blocking if possible.

Report Cyber Harassment

How to Report Cyber Harassment

If you're experiencing online harassment, it's important to document, report, and seek support. Use this form to document your experience. Your information will be kept confidential.

For immediate threats or emergency situations, please contact local law enforcement directly.
