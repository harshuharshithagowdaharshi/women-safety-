Legal & Social Awareness

Understand your legal rights, access supportive communities, and connect with mental health resources.

Legal Rights Information

Constitutional Rights

The Constitution guarantees equality (Article 14), prohibition of discrimination (Article 15), and right to life and personal liberty (Article 21) which are fundamental to women's legal protections.

Equal Pay Rights

The Equal Remuneration Act mandates equal pay for equal work regardless of gender. Employers must provide equal compensation and cannot discriminate in hiring practices.

Property Rights

The Hindu Succession Act and personal laws guarantee women's right to inheritance and property. Women have equal rights to ancestral property and can be joint legal heirs.

Right to Dignity

Women have the right to dignity and freedom from harassment. This includes protection from obscenity in advertisements, indecent representation, and cyber harassment.

Laws Protecting Women

Domestic Violence Act

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act (2005) provides protection against abuse within the home, including physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse. It offers options for protection orders, residence orders, and monetary relief.

Sexual Harassment Laws

The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act (2013) protects women from harassment in professional settings. Additionally, Section 354A of the IPC criminalizes sexual harassment and unwelcome sexual advances.

Maternity Benefit Act

The Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act (2017) provides for 26 weeks of paid maternity leave and requires establishments with 50+ employees to have crèche facilities, ensuring women's right to work while raising children.

Criminal Law Provisions

The Indian Penal Code has specific provisions against rape (Sec. 376), stalking (Sec. 354D), voyeurism (Sec. 354C), acid attacks (Sec. 326A & 326B), and trafficking (Sec. 370 & 370A) providing comprehensive legal protection.

Support Groups & NGO Directory

Legal Aid Organizations

Organizations like the Legal Services Authority provide free legal assistance to women. They help with filing complaints, obtaining protection orders, and navigating the legal system. Contact: 1516 (National Legal Services Authority)

Crisis Intervention Centers

One-stop crisis centers provide medical, legal, psychological and rehabilitation services to women affected by violence. These centers operate 24/7 and are often located in hospitals. Contact: 181 (Women Helpline)

Domestic Violence Support

Organizations such as Shakti Shalini and YWCA provide shelter, counseling, and support to survivors of domestic violence. They also operate helplines for emergency assistance. Shakti Shalini Helpline: 011-24373737

Women's Rights NGOs

Organizations like Jagori, Action India, and CREA work to promote women's rights through advocacy, research, and direct support services. They offer resources on gender-based violence and discrimination. Jagori Helpline: 011-26692700

Mental Health Support

Why Mental Health Matters

Mental health is as important as physical health. Trauma, stress, and anxiety can significantly impact your wellbeing. Seeking support is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Our mental health support form connects you with professional counselors specialized in trauma, anxiety, depression, and other mental health concerns. All information shared is strictly confidential.
