Workplace Safety

Learn about your rights, policies and measures to ensure a safe and respectful workplace environment for women.

Sexual Harassment Policy

Definition and Types

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature that interferes with work performance or creates a hostile environment.

Employee Rights

All employees have the right to work in an environment free from harassment. You have the right to report incidents without fear of retaliation and to expect a prompt investigation.

Reporting Procedure

Know your company's reporting procedure. Reports should be made to supervisors, HR, or designated personnel. Document incidents, including dates, times, locations, and witnesses.

Bystander Intervention

Learn how to be an active bystander. Intervene when you witness harassment by speaking up, offering support to the target, or reporting the behavior to appropriate personnel.

POSH Act Information

About the POSH Act

The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 protects women from sexual harassment at the workplace. It mandates employers to provide mechanisms for addressing complaints.

Internal Complaints Committee

Every organization with 10 or more employees must have an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC). Know your ICC members and how to approach them if needed.

Investigation Process

Under the POSH Act, the ICC must complete investigations within 90 days and submit recommendations within 10 days after that. The entire process is confidential to protect all parties involved.

Safe Transportation Services

Company Transportation

Many companies offer safe transportation services, especially for employees working night shifts. Inquire about these services with your HR department or administration.

Travel Safety Tips

Share your travel plans with trusted colleagues or family. Use reliable transportation services, check vehicle details before entering, and track your journey using safety apps.

Women-Only Transportation

Some cities offer women-only transportation options such as women-only cabs or separate sections in public transport. Familiarize yourself with these options in your area.

Transportation Rights

Companies operating between 8:00 PM and 6:00 AM are often required by law to provide transportation for women employees. Know your rights and company policies regarding safe transport.
